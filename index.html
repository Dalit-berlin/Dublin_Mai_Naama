Machina Craft

An installation of live electronic music within a workshop for instrument repair.

  • Format Live electronic music installation
  • Structure Three movements
  • Timing Approx. 20 minutes each, with 10-minute pauses

Welcome

What you see on the screen and hear through the headphones is a live broadcast, unfolding in real time in another room. You are invited to follow the signs to Room 21 and experience it live, up close.

The electronic music you hear is rooted in this very space, recorded within the workshop and shaped by its tools, gestures, and sounds.

Event Structure

The event unfolds in three parts. Each part lasts approximately 20 minutes, with a 10-minute pause in between.

Like a sonata, Machina Craft is composed in three movements.

First Movement

The first movement is divided into three sections: an introduction to the artists, each immersed in their own practice; a duet between the tools of their sound of craft and electronic music; and a layered composition where boundaries begin to dissolve.

Tools, the music they generate, electronic textures, and vocal improvisation intertwine with recorded and live text, forming a shifting composition.

Second Movement

In a classical sonata, the second movement often takes the form of an andante: slower, more contemplative. Here, too, the pulse softens. Time stretches, rhythm loosens, and the atmosphere opens into a gentler, more spacious breath.

Third Movement

The cycle closes. The clarinet is reassembled, piece by piece, returning to form while carrying within it the traces of all that has passed.

Follow the signs to Room 21 to move from the broadcast into the live space.
